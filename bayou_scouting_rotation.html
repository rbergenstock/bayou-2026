📋 Why Scouting Matters

Scouting is the single biggest competitive advantage a team can have at a regional. Rankings and win-loss records can be deceiving — a team that went 2-7 in quals might have been dragged down by weak alliance partners, while a 7-2 team might have been carried by strong ones. Your scouting data tells the real story.

At ARLI, our scouting data predicted match outcomes with 82% accuracy. That gave us real intel for match strategy and alliance selection. If we do our jobs in the stands at Bayou, we'll know the field better than anyone else in the building by Saturday morning.

🎯 Top Scouting Tips

In the Stands — Match Scouting

Watch ONE robot, not the match. You're assigned to a single robot in a single station (Red 1, Red 2, etc.). Ignore everything else on the field. Your job is to be an expert on that one robot for that one match.
Watch the WHOLE match. Don't look at your tablet until the buzzer sounds. Watch first, record after. It's better to remember what happened and enter it accurately than to miss something because you were typing mid-match.
Be honest, not generous. If a robot didn't do much, say so. If they broke down, record it. Sugar-coating data hurts us during alliance selection. Report what you see, not what you think the team wants to hear.
Record consistency, not just highlights. A robot that scores 3 fuel every match is more valuable than one that scored 10 once and 0 three times. Note whether a robot is reliable — do they do the same thing every match, or are they streaky?
Note defense and penalties. Does the robot play defense? Are they effective at it? Do they draw fouls? A robot that causes penalties is a liability even if they score well.

In the Pits — Pit Scouting

Be polite and professional. You're visiting another team's workspace. Introduce yourself: "Hi, we're Team 16, Bomb Squad. Mind if we ask a few questions about your robot?" Gracious Professionalism applies everywhere.
Take a photo of the robot. Pictures help us remember robots during data review and draft night. Get a clear shot showing the full robot, especially the intake and scoring mechanism.
Look around the pit. Is the team organized? Do they have spare parts? Is their robot under repair? A team with a clean, organized pit usually has a reliable robot. A pit full of parts and panic usually means problems on the field.
Ask about auto. "What does your robot do in autonomous?" is the single most valuable pit question. Auto points are free points, and knowing who has a good auto helps predict match scores.
Ask about climb. "Can you climb? What level? How fast?" Endgame is worth a lot of points in REBUILT. Know who can and who can't.

General Tips from the FRC Community

Less is more. According to FIRST's scouting guide, the more you ask a scout to track, the less accurate the data gets. We've designed BombsAway to focus on the key metrics — don't try to track everything, track what matters.
Debrief with the drive team after every match. They see things from the field that scouts can't see from the stands. Quick 30-second check-ins add qualitative context to your data.
Review data at the end of each day. Friday night is critical — that's when we run OneRing on the hotel TV, review all our data, look for patterns, and start building our pick list.
Bring snacks and water to the stands. Scouting is a marathon, not a sprint. You'll be in your seat for 45-minute shifts. Stay fed, stay hydrated, stay focused.
📱 BombsAway Scout App — Quick Reference

Getting Started

Your tablet is your tool. Each scout gets a Lenovo M10 tablet. It stays with your seat position (Red 1, Red 2, Red 3, Blue 1, Blue 2, Blue 3). When you sit down for a shift, pick up the tablet at that seat.
The team auto-fills. When a match starts, the tablet already knows which team you're scouting based on your seat position and the match schedule. You don't need to enter the team number — just verify it's correct at the top of the screen.

During a Match

Auto period: Watch your robot during the 15-second auto. Did they move? Score? Leave the zone? After auto ends, fill in the auto section on the tablet.
Teleop period: Watch your robot score. Track fuel volleys, where they're picking up and scoring. Note if they play defense or get defended. Fill in teleop after the period or during breaks in action.
Endgame: Watch for climb attempts. Did they climb? What level? Did they fail? How fast? Fill in the endgame section after the buzzer.
Comments: Use the free-text comment field for anything that doesn't fit the checkboxes. "Broke down at 0:45" or "Great defense on 4635" or "Tipped over during climb" — these comments are gold during data review.

After the Match

Hit Submit. This sends your data via Bluetooth to the Master tablet (Mr. B's tablet at the end of the row). You'll see a confirmation. If it fails, it saves locally and will sync next time.
Do NOT clear the app. Just hit submit and it resets for the next match automatically. If something goes wrong, tell Mr. B — don't try to fix it yourself.

Pit Scouting Mode

Switch to Pit Scout mode from the app menu. Select the team you're visiting from the dropdown. Fill in drivetrain type, scoring capabilities, auto routine, climb ability, and take a photo. Hit Submit when done.
Don't forget the photo! The camera button is at the bottom of the pit scout form. One clear photo of the full robot — it makes a huge difference during Friday night data review.
